About Me

Technology is personal.
So is how I build it.

I've spent my career building technology that connects people and solves meaningful problems. From Goldman Sachs in New York to Google, I've worked across products from Wallet to Search and Ads, and now I lead Google Ghana — helping shape research and innovation across the African continent.

My focus has always been on the intersection of technology, society, and human potential: how we can design systems that don't just work, but that uplift. Whether it's enabling voice technology in African languages, building AI-powered tools for small businesses, or putting computers in the hands of students who need them — the thread is the same.

Outside of Google, I run Mina Logic, an AI-powered app development studio. I'm also the CTO of Otto Couture, a bespoke African fashion house founded by my best friend Asafo-Adjei Amoakohene. When he asked me to build an app for his tailoring business, that project grew into Bespoke — the AI-powered management platform that now serves thousands of bespoke tailors. I'm also a father who believes the best technology is the kind that makes the next generation more capable than the last.

African Languages

Open Source · CC-BY-4.0

WAXAL: A Large-Scale Multilingual African Language Speech Corpus

Co-authored an open-source speech corpus supporting 20+ Sub-Saharan African languages. What started as an open-source toolkit for crowdsourcing speech data — now used by organizations like Sunbird.AI to collect speech for East African languages — planted the seeds for WAXAL, which has grown into one of the largest multilingual African language datasets ever published.

Giving Back

The Emmanuel Nii Azuma Nelson
Computer Lab

"If something must be done, it must be done well."
— Emmanuel Nii Azuma Nelson, my father

When I was young, my dad gave me his work computer and went back to using a typewriter. That single act changed my life. It gave me access to a world of possibility that shaped everything I've built since.

The Emmanuel Nii Azuma Nelson Computer Lab at Liberty No. 2 High School in Adabraka, Accra, is my way of passing that gift forward. Dedicated in May 2026, the lab gives students access to computers and digital learning resources — because I know firsthand that a single computer can change a life.
