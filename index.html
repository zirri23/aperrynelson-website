About Me

Technology is personal.
So is how I build it.

I've spent my career building technology that connects people and solves meaningful problems. At Google, I've worked across products from Wallet to Search and Ads, and now I lead Google Ghana — helping shape research and innovation across the African continent.

My focus has always been on the intersection of technology, society, and human potential: how we can design systems that don't just work, but that uplift. Whether it's enabling voice technology in African languages, building AI-powered tools for small businesses, or putting computers in the hands of students who need them — the thread is the same.

I'm a Ghanaian building from Ghana, for Ghana, and for the world. I run Mina Logic, an AI-powered app development studio. I founded Otto Couture, a bespoke African fashion house. And I'm a father who believes that the best technology is the kind that makes the next generation more capable than the last.

Research & Innovation

Advancing AI for
African Languages

Ensuring that the next generation of AI understands and speaks the languages of a billion people.

Open Source · CC-BY-4.0

WAXAL: A Large-Scale Multilingual African Language Speech Corpus

Co-authored an open-source speech dataset supporting 20+ Sub-Saharan African languages with over 1,250 hours of transcribed speech. Named from the Wolof word for "speak," WAXAL enables ASR and TTS systems to work in languages that were previously invisible to AI.

Authenticating Financial Accounts

Co-invented a system for securely associating payment accounts with users for online and mobile transactions. The system uses authorization holds and credential verification to authenticate account ownership — foundational technology for Google Wallet/Payments.

Giving Back

The Emmanuel Nii Azuma Nelson
Computer Lab

"If something must be done, it must be done well."

At Liberty No. 2 High School in Adabraka, Accra, students now have access to computers and digital learning resources through the Emmanuel Nii Azuma Nelson Computer Lab — dedicated in May 2026.

I believe that access to technology is a fundamental equalizer. Every student deserves the chance to learn, create, and dream with the same tools available to students anywhere in the world. This lab is a small step toward that vision — but every step matters.
